CineScope: Personal Cinema Analytics

Years of curated watchlists and a carefully built movie collection, transformed into a data project to understand my own cinematic patterns.

I’ve been exporting IMDb lists, maintaining spreadsheets, and building databases of my films for years. CineScope is where I turned that private obsession into a structured analytics pipeline: who I watch, which stories I return to, how my taste evolves across decades, and where the hidden gaps in my collection are.

2,000+ Films in personal dataset
30+ Visual analytics views
5+ External data sources

From Lifelong Lists to a Data Project

CineScope is built on top of years of curated lists: personal watch history, IMDb exports, local collection databases, and quiet notes about what I love. I pulled all of this together, enriched it with external sources (IMDb, TMDb, OMDb, DoesTheDogDie, Wikidata), and created a pipeline that treats my own film life like a research dataset.

Instead of asking “what should I watch?”, this project asks deeper questions: What do I actually watch? Where are my biases? Which genres and eras define my taste? Who shows up over and over again when I’m not paying attention?

My Taste, Over Time

The first layer of CineScope looks at ratings, decades, and runtimes. It turns vague intuition (“I like classic films”, “I’m not into very long movies”) into measurable patterns.

Genres, Hybrids & Evolution

I’ve always said I love specific genres – horror, noir, thrillers, classic Hollywood. CineScope translates that into concrete distributions and hybrid genre patterns.

Who I Watch: People & Representation

One of the aims of this project is to move beyond “what films” into “who”: which performers, ensembles, and patterns of representation are actually present in my viewing.

Careers, Longevity & Trajectories

Because the dataset is enriched with years and filmographies, I can look at careers instead of just isolated performances: how long actors stay present, when they peak, and how quality moves with them.

Behind the Camera & Awards

CineScope also tracks the ecosystem behind the films: directors, studios, awards, and how critical recognition overlaps with my own ratings.

How Critics and Sources See My Films

Because the project merges multiple external sources, I can compare how I rate films against IMDb, Rotten Tomatoes, Metacritic and others.

Patterns, Gaps & What Comes Next

At the final stage, CineScope uses patterns in the enriched data to suggest what I might want to watch next, and to highlight where my collection is still narrow.

Under the Hood

CineScope is built as a modular Python pipeline combining IMDb non-commercial datasets, APIs (TMDb, OMDb, DoesTheDogDie, Wikidata), and a dense network of CSV, Parquet, and SQLite layers. The same tools I use in research are pointed at something extremely personal: my own watch history and collection.

The repository shows the full pipeline: data ingestion, enrichment scripts, analysis batches, visual exports, and an early UI prototype exploring search and browsing on top of the enriched data.
